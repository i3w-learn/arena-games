## "Wrappers over MCQs": The Failure of Current EdTech

### The Core Problem

Most educational games are just forced Multiple Choice Questions disguised with a thin layer of superficial graphics. The "wrappers" themselves lack the addictive core game loops that make mainstream titles compelling.

### The Market Gap

Market saturation exists for young children (K-5), but the market for **grown-up, sophisticated educational games** is either non-existent or severely underdeveloped.

### The Complexity Barrier

Educational games require a rare fusion of complex game design expertise and deep pedagogical science—two domains rarely found in the same organization.

## A Portfolio Built for Experimentation and Scale

Our strategic roadmap follows three distinct tracks of evolution, allowing us to test, iterate, and scale at different complexity levels simultaneously.

### Track 1: Mini Games

Bite-sized, granular, and stripped-down experiences with zero bloat. No meta-economy or social features—pure mechanics experimentation.

**Purpose:** Total developer freedom to experiment with control schemes and core gameplay loops.

### Track 2: Wrapper Games

Combining mini game worlds with narrative progression, 2D avatars, and educational content integration.

**Includes:** Casual and hybrid-casual games with strong educational tilt for Grades 6-12.

### Track 3: Super-Wrapper

The ultimate evolution: A massive platform aggregator (The Arena) unifying experiences across tracks.

**Scope:** Currently beyond immediate experimental horizon—reserved for post-validation phase.

## Combat + JEE/NEET Revision: Injecting MCQs into High-Octane Gameplay

### The Strategic Insight

Kids use games to **escape study**. Rather than fighting this behavior, we inject educational content directly into gameplay that already engages them.

### Target Demographic

Grade 9+ students preparing for competitive exams like JEE and NEET in India—high-stakes testing environments with significant parental expenditure.

### The Visual Differentiation

Headline-worthy graphics mimicking AAA mobile titles, not kindergarten-friendly "kiddy" art. Sophisticated UI/UX that resonates with older teens.

> **Revenue Opportunity:** JEE/NEET prep market represents billions in spending across coaching, materials, and digital tools—untapped for gamification at scale.

### Gameplay Structure

Combat scenarios (arena battles, boss fights) interrupted by randomized MCQ checkpoints. Correct answers build combo multipliers; incorrect answers incur temporary penalties.

Progression gated by cumulative score thresholds, ensuring knowledge mastery before advancing.

## "Train Your Brain": The High School Survival Experiment

### The Hook
Disguised as viral brain training app trending on TikTok/Reels.

### The Theme
High school zombie apocalypse—All of Us Are Dead meets study survival.

### The Loop
Woodoku-style physics puzzles driving idle RPG progression from Cup Heroes.

The "Train Your Brain" title serves as bait—the underlying mechanics deliver actual skill development while maintaining addictive engagement. Students believe they're consuming entertainment while developing cognitive skills transferable to academic contexts.
